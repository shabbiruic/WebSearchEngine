Introduction to chemical information, including the use of databases for searching chemical information and the use of molecular modeling and related computational systems to determine calculated properties of chemical substances. Introduction to the principles of inorganic chemistry. Structural and descriptive chemistry of the main-group elements. Course Information: 2 undergraduate hours. 3 graduate hours. Prerequisite(s): Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 314 ; and Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 340 or Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 342 ; or consent of the instructor.    CHEMÂ 415.  Inorganic Chemistry Laboratory.  0-4 hours.  Advanced inorganic chemistry laboratory. Preparative methods, Schlenk techniques, dry box, Fourier-transform infra-red and UV-visible  spectroscopy, crystal growth. Course Information: 3 undergraduate hours. 4 graduate hours. Prerequisite(s): Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 314 . Class Schedule Information: To be properly registered, students must enroll in one Laboratory and one Lecture.    CHEMÂ 416.  Inorganic Chemistry II.  3 or 4 hours.  Structural and descriptive chemistry of the transition elements. Course Information: 3 undergraduate hours. 4 graduate hours. Prerequisite(s): CHEMÂ 414 .    CHEMÂ 421.  Instrumental Analysis.  0-4 hours.  A survey of contemporary instrumentation for chemical analysis. Emphasis on fundamentals of instrumental methods with actual experience on typical equipment. Includes a weekly three-hour laboratory. Course Information: 3 undergraduate hours. 4 graduate hours. Prerequisite(s): Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 222 ; or Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 118 . Class Schedule Information: To be properly registered, students must enroll in one Laboratory and one Lecture-Discussion.    CHEMÂ 432.  Advanced Organic Chemistry.  2 or 3 hours.  Rigorous treatment of the principles upon which modern organic chemistry is developed. Course Information: 2 undergraduate hours. 3 graduate hours. Prerequisite(s): Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 333 ; and Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 340 or Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 342 .    CHEMÂ 444.  Spectroscopy in Chemistry and Biochemistry.  3 or 4 hours.  Applications of theory and experiment to the spectroscopy of molecules and biological macromolecules. Course Information: 3 undergraduate hours. 4 graduate hours. Prerequisite(s): Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 346 or Grade of C or better in CHEMÂ 344 .    CHEMÂ 448.  Statistical Thermodynamics.  3 or 4 hours.  Introduction to statistical mechanics, partition functions, chemical equilibrium, ensembles, fluctuations, real gases, Einstein and Debye models of solids, magnetic materials, electrolytes, introduction to liquids. Course Information: 3 undergraduate hours. 4 graduate hours. Prerequisite(s): CHEMÂ 346 .    CHEMÂ 452.  Biochemistry I.  4 hours.  Chemistry of proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates and lipids. Course Information: Same as BIOSÂ 452 . Prerequisite(s): Credit or concurrent registration in CHEMÂ 234 . Class Schedule Information:  To be properly registered, students must enroll in one Discussion/Recitation and one Lecture.    CHEMÂ 454.  Biochemistry II.  4 hours.  Continues Chemistry 452. Carbohydrate and lipid metabolism, electron transport. Metabolism of amino acids, nucleic acids, proteins. Biosynthesis of macromolecules and regulation of macromolecular synthesis. Course Information: Same as BIOSÂ 454 . Prerequisite(s): BIOSÂ 452 or CHEMÂ 452 . Class Schedule Information:  To be properly registered, students must enroll in one Discussion/Recitation and one Lecture.    CHEMÂ 455.  Biochemistry Laboratory.  3 hours.  Introduction to modern biochemistry & molecular biology research. Includes recombinant DNA techniques, protein purification, site-directed mutagenesis, polymerase chain reaction, enzyme kinetics, protein structure data analysis & molecular graphics.  Course Information: Prerequisite(s): CHEMÂ 222 and CHEMÂ 452 .  Class Schedule Information:  To be properly registered, students must enroll in one Laboratory and one Laboratory-Discussion. General principles and selected studies of phenolic compounds, terpenes, alkaloids, and other interesting natural products. Course Information: Same as BIOSÂ 416 . 3 undergraduate hours. 4 graduate hours. Prerequisite(s): One year of organic chemistry.    CHEMÂ 458.  Biotechnology and Drug Discovery.  3 or 4 hours. Educational Practice with Seminar I.  6 hours.  The first half of a two-segment sequence of practice teaching, including seminar, to meet certification requirements for teaching in grades six through twelve. Course Information: Same as PHYSÂ 475 . Prerequisite(s): Senior standing or above; or approval of the department. Recommended background: Knowledge of first-year college physics and chemistry. Course Information: Prerequisite(s): CHEMÂ 432 or the equivalent, and credit or concurrent registration in CHEMÂ 532 .    CHEMÂ 518.  Advanced Inorganic Chemistry III.  4 hours.  Synthesis, structure, bonding, and properties of solid state materials. Course Information: Prerequisite(s): CHEMÂ 416 or the equivalent or consent of the instructor.    CHEMÂ 519.  Special Topics in Inorganic Chemistry.  3-4 hours.  Lectures on topics not represented in regularly scheduled courses. Course Information: May be repeated. Prerequisite(s): Graduate standing or consent of instructor.    CHEMÂ 520.  Literature Seminar in Analytical Chemistry.  1 hour.  Discussion of analytical chemical research from the current literature. Emphasis upon student presentations. Course Information: Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory grading only.    CHEMÂ 522.  Techniques in Mass Spectrometry and Surface Analysis.  4 hours.  Various methods in mass spectrometry. Course Information: Prerequisite(s): CHEMÂ 421 or the equivalent.    CHEMÂ 524.  Optical Spectroscopies in Analytical Chemistry.  4 hours.  Theory and experimental methods in infrared, ultraviolet and visible spectroscopies, both absorption and emission. Course Information: Prerequisite(s): CHEMÂ 346 and CHEMÂ 421 ; or consent of the instructor.    CHEMÂ 526.  NMR Spectroscopy in Analytical Chemistry.  4 hours.  Principles governing one- and multi-dimensional nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectroscopy; applications of NMR to chemical analysis. Course Information: Prerequisite(s): CHEMÂ 346 and CHEMÂ 421 ; or the equivalent or consent of the instructor.    CHEMÂ 528.  Chemical Separations.  4 hours.  Fundamentals and recent advances in techniques and technologies for the separation of chemical substances, including both chromotographic and electrophoretic methods. Discussion of organic chemical research from the current literature. Emphasis upon student presentations. Course Information: Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory grading only.
